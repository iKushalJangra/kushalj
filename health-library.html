Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about medical tests, procedures, and health conditions.

How should I prepare for an MRI scan?

Preparation for an MRI scan depends on the area being examined. Generally, you may be asked to:

  • Remove all metal objects (jewelry, watches, hairpins)
  • Wear comfortable clothing without metal zippers or buttons
  • Inform your doctor if you have any metal implants or devices
  • Follow specific instructions about eating or drinking beforehand

Your radiology team will provide detailed preparation instructions based on your specific scan.

What's the difference between a CT scan and an MRI?

CT (Computed Tomography) and MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) are both imaging tests but use different technologies:

  • CT scans use X-rays to create cross-sectional images and are better for viewing bones, detecting cancers, and examining chest/abdominal issues.
  • MRIs use magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of soft tissues like the brain, spinal cord, muscles, and ligaments.
  • CT scans are typically faster (5-10 minutes) while MRIs take longer (15-90 minutes).
  • CT involves radiation exposure while MRI does not.

Your doctor will recommend the most appropriate test based on your medical needs.

Is radiation from imaging tests dangerous?

The small amount of radiation used in diagnostic imaging tests like X-rays and CT scans is generally considered safe when medically necessary. However:

  • Radiation exposure is cumulative over a lifetime
  • Modern equipment uses the lowest possible dose
  • Benefits of accurate diagnosis typically outweigh risks
  • Pregnant women should inform their doctor as radiation may affect the fetus

MRIs and ultrasounds do not use ionizing radiation. Discuss any concerns with your healthcare provider.

How long does it take to get imaging results?

Result turnaround times vary depending on:

  • Type of exam: Routine X-rays may be read same day, while complex MRIs may take 1-2 days
  • Facility: Hospital-based imaging often has faster results than outpatient centers
  • Urgency: Emergency cases are prioritized
  • Your provider: Some doctors receive results before patients and will contact you

Most facilities provide results within 24-48 hours. You can typically access results through your patient portal once they're finalized.
